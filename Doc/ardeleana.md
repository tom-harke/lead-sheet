# Names, Notation

The focus is on those tune which feel like they have tempo changes in each bar of music.
Some transcribers write them in 7/16 time, where 7=3+4,
  and 4 notes are played in the '3' part,
  4 in the '4' part.
Other transcribers write them in 10/16 time, where 10=4+6,
  and 4 quarter notes are played in the '4' part,
  4 dotted quarter notes in the '6' part.

I don't know the proper name for them, but the following 2 terms have high correlation with this rhythm
 - ardeleana which translates to 'transylvanian', referring to the origin
 - învârtită which refers to the rotation in the dance, however there are several învârtită in even time signatures
