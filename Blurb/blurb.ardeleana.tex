\section{Rhythm}
Different measures may have a different feels
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
\item speeding up and slowing down
\item lesnoto
\end{itemize}
There 2 variants 7 and 10.
\subsection{10/16}
\begin{center}
\lilypond{\time 10/16 d'4 d'8.[ d']}
\end{center}
which subdivides to
\begin{center}
\lilypond{\time 10/16 d'16[ d' d' d'] d'16.[ d' d' d']}
\end{center}
with a lesnoto-like syncopation
\begin{center}
\lilypond{\time 10/16 d'8.[ d'16] d'8.[ d']}
\end{center}


\subsection{7/16}
\begin{center}
\lilypond{\time 7/16 d'8. d'8[ d']}
\end{center}
which subdivides to
\begin{center}
\lilypond{\time 7/16 \times 3/4 {d'16 d' d' d'} d'16 d' d' d'}
\end{center}
with a lesnoto alternative
\begin{center}
\lilypond{\time 7/16 d'8[ d'16] d'8[ d']}
\end{center}

\section{Naming}
The name of this rhythm is not known.
There are 2 descriptive terms that correlate to it:
{\bf ardeleană} which seems to mean {\bf transylvanian}
(ardel means Transylvania in Romanian, érdely in Hungarian),
and {\bf învârtită} which seems to mean {\bf turning}.
There are many învârtitas (and ardeleanas?) in different rhythms.
